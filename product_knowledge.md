# Developers Institute — Product Knowledge Reference

Consolidated from: 4 official brochures (Full Stack & AI, Data Analytics, AI for Business, GenAI & Machine Learning) and the internal Sales FAQ (~450 Aircall calls analyzed, Mar–Jul 2026). Used to keep Vapi personas and rep training grounded in accurate, current facts.

**Two stats are explicitly unresolved internally (flagged by the FAQ itself) — do not present either as settled fact in training material without checking with management:**
- Job placement rate: brochures publish **75%** ("more than 75% of our students found a job within six months") — reps on calls have also cited up to 91%. **We use 75% everywhere below since it's the officially published figure.**
- Internship → hire rate: cited between 50% and 85% on calls, no official published number. **We do not quote a number for this anywhere.**

## Courses

| Course | Full-time | Part-time | Flex-time | Best for |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Full Stack & AI | 3 months | 6 months | self-paced | Career changers wanting the broadest option (JS, React, Node, Python, SQL + GenAI/agentic AI) |
| Data Analytics | 3 months | 6 months | self-paced | Analytical/business background wanting a faster, more focused entry (Python, SQL, Tableau/Power BI, ML basics) |
| GenAI & Machine Learning | 3 months | 6 months | self-paced | Some technical appetite, wants to build with LLMs/AI directly (deep learning, NLP, RAG, agentic AI) |
| AI for Business | 4 weeks (FT) | 8 weeks (PT) | — | Business professionals who want to use AI at work, not become developers (strategy, no-code, governance) |

All: no prior technical background required, basic English required, licensed by the Israeli Ministry of Labor. Prep phase (~20h self-paced on the Octopus platform) before day one so nobody starts behind.

**Schedules**: Full-time Sun–Thu, ~09:30–16:30. Part-time 3 evenings/week (Sun, Tue, Thu — AI for Business uses Mon/Wed instead), 18:00–21:00. On-site (Bezalel 8, Ramat Gan, near the train station), fully online, or hybrid — switchable day by day. All classes recorded.

**Certification requirement**: complete ~70% of mandatory exercises + pass final project(s) to get the diploma.

**Cohorts start every 4–8 weeks.** A start date should never be the reason a lead goes cold — offer the sooner cohort with catch-up, or lock the deposit for the next one.

- Deposit: **₪4,000**, secures the seat and locks in any discount.
- Up to **10 installments** possible, including credit-card installments (~3.5% processing fee on card payments).
- **Early-bird discount ~₪3,000** if signing roughly a month before the start date (or a stated deadline) — the main real urgency lever on calls. Always give the exact expiry date and book a follow-up before it passes.

## Funding stack (stack multiple, don't present just one)

1. **Government voucher — ₪7,000.** For new immigrants (Olim), the unemployed, and career-changers aged ~47–48+. (Ministry of Labor / Aliyah & Integration programs.)
2. **Ogen no-interest loan — up to ₪20,000.** Nonprofit-funded; repayment starts only after the graduate finds a job. The single most effective answer to price objections — reframes the course as "pay when it works."
3. **External scholarships — up to ₪12,000**, including community-specific ones (e.g. for religious candidates from the Jerusalem area).
4. **Employer sponsorship**: some prospects get their employer to cover it — offer a syllabus/invoice/letter to support the internal request.
5. Stack with the **early-bird discount** and **installments** on top.

**Unemployment benefits interaction (important, decision-critical)**: evening part-time generally lets candidates keep collecting unemployment; daytime full-time may conflict with it. This is a real trade-off prospects weigh (part-time preserves benefits but takes longer; full-time is faster but may end benefits early) — and unemployment status can itself unlock voucher eligibility.

## Career support

- Dedicated career coach: CV, LinkedIn, interview training, storytelling.
- Network of 100+ hiring partner companies.
- Optional ~2-month **unpaid internship** with partner companies after the course (bridges the "no Israeli experience" problem). No official hire-rate number to quote (see caveat above).
- **Job placement: 75%** of graduates found a job within six months (published figure).
- Typical junior tech salary quoted on calls: ~₪20,000/month, varies by role; climbs after 1–2 years experience.
- **"Bootcamp for Life"**: graduates can take future DI courses for free — position as an entry ticket to ongoing upskilling, not a one-off purchase.
- Notable graduate employers: eBay, Payoneer, HP, Check Point, Yotpo, Wix, Atera, AppsFlyer, Bank Hapoalim, accessiBe, and others.

## Enrollment process (the concrete next-step path)

1. **Skills assessment test** — short, sent by email right after the call. Not an elimination filter, a fit check. Follow up within 1–2 days if not done.
2. **Syllabus & info pack** — sent immediately by email/WhatsApp with course details and funding info.
3. **Funding applications** — submitted in parallel (voucher/loan/scholarship); rep checks status on follow-up.
4. **Agreement & deposit** — ₪4,000 locks the seat and any discount.
5. **Prep phase** — ~20h self-paced material before day one.

Rule from winning calls: **book the next concrete step before hanging up.** Deals ending on "I'll get back to you" with no booked follow-up rarely close.

## Objection bank (expanded, from real call patterns)

**"Am I too old for this?"** (very common, ages ~40–55): name it directly, don't brush it aside. Many students are 35+. The over-47 government funding is itself proof the state backs older career-changers. Employers hire for skill/portfolio, and the career coach specifically preps candidates to present experience as an asset.

**"Won't AI take these jobs anyway?"**: reframe — AI is transforming the roles, which is why AI/ML tools are now in every track. The risk isn't AI replacing analysts/developers; it's professionals who don't use AI being replaced by those who do.

**"I work full-time / have kids, can I manage?"**: point to part-time evenings, recordings, hybrid, and the Flex track. Be honest it requires real commitment. Concrete scheduling detail (which evenings, what hours, catch-up policy) is what actually persuades parents.

**"It's a lot of money and a long commitment, I need to think."**: isolate the real blocker (money / time / confidence / partner sign-off), answer that specific blocker, attach a real deadline (early-bird expiry, cohort filling), book the follow-up before hanging up.

**"Can I talk to past students?"**: have 2–3 graduate stories ready matching common personas (Oleh career-changer, parent studying part-time, 45+ switcher).

**"Is a bootcamp certificate really enough? Don't employers want a degree?"**: Israeli tech employers hire for demonstrated hands-on skill — portfolio, internship experience, up-to-date stack matter more than a degree for junior roles. Several graduates with degrees (even PhDs) enrolled because academic knowledge wasn't landing them jobs.

**"Which course is right for me?"**: a buying signal — take the lead. Career change, broad options → Full Stack. Analytical/business background, faster entry → Data Analytics. Technical appetite, wants to build with AI → GenAI & ML. Business pro who wants to use AI at work → AI for Business.

**"Is it really in English? My English isn't great."**: yes, everything is in English; conversational level is enough, materials/recordings allow reviewing at one's own pace. Comes up constantly with Olim and international students.

**"I'm abroad / traveling during the course."**: can join online from abroad and switch to on-site later; recordings cover shorter absences (weddings, relocations, etc. — get concrete dates rather than leaving it vague).
